Post-Impressionism

The Starry Night

Vincent van Gogh

One of the most recognized pieces in modern art, The Starry Night depicts a swirling night sky over a small French village. Painted from memory during van Gogh's stay at the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, this masterpiece showcases the artist's unique style of bold, dramatic brushstrokes and vivid imagination. The cypress tree in the foreground and the vibrant stars create a dreamlike atmosphere that has captivated viewers for over a century.

Year Created
1889
Medium
Oil on Canvas
Location
MoMA, New York
Dimensions
73.7 × 92.1 cm

Renaissance

Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci

The world's most famous painting, the Mona Lisa captivates with her enigmatic smile and mysterious gaze. Da Vinci's masterful use of sfumato technique creates a dreamlike quality, while the subject's expression has fascinated viewers for centuries. The portrait is believed to be of Lisa Gherardini, wife of a Florentine merchant. Its fame stems not only from the technical mastery but also from the countless interpretations of her expression, making it the most valuable and visited artwork in the world.

Year Created
1503-1519
Medium
Oil on Poplar
Location
Louvre, Paris
Dimensions
77 × 53 cm

Ukiyo-e

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

Katsushika Hokusai

This iconic Japanese woodblock print depicts an enormous wave threatening boats off the coast of Kanagawa. With Mount Fuji visible in the background, Hokusai brilliantly captures the power and beauty of nature in a single, dynamic composition. The print's innovative use of Prussian blue pigment and its bold composition revolutionized Japanese art and profoundly influenced Western artists including Vincent van Gogh and Claude Monet, becoming one of the most recognizable images in world art.

Year Created
1830-1833
Medium
Woodblock Print
Location
Multiple Collections
Dimensions
25 × 37 cm

Expressionism

The Scream

Edvard Munch

An iconic symbol of modern anxiety and existential dread, The Scream depicts a figure with an agonized expression against a tumultuous orange sky. Munch created this powerful work to represent "the scream of nature," inspired by a walk during which he experienced overwhelming anxiety. The swirling sky and distorted landscape capture universal human emotions of fear and isolation in a deeply personal and revolutionary style that pioneered Expressionism and continues to resonate with contemporary audiences.

Year Created
1893
Medium
Oil & Pastel
Location
National Gallery, Oslo
Dimensions
91 × 73.5 cm

Dutch Golden Age

Girl with a Pearl Earring

Johannes Vermeer

Often called the "Dutch Mona Lisa," this captivating portrait features a young woman wearing an exotic turban and large pearl earring. Vermeer's masterful treatment of light and subtle color palette create an intimate moment frozen in time, with the subject's enigmatic gaze drawing viewers into her world. The painting is a tronie, a Dutch portrait type focusing on facial expression rather than identity. The luminous pearl and the girl's direct yet mysterious look have made this one of the most beloved portraits in art history.

Year Created
1665
Medium
Oil on Canvas
Location
Mauritshuis, The Hague
Dimensions
44.5 × 39 cm

Early Renaissance

The Birth of Venus

Sandro Botticelli

This mythological masterpiece depicts the goddess Venus emerging from the sea as a fully grown woman, arriving at the shore on a shell. Botticelli's graceful composition, flowing lines, and ethereal beauty exemplify Renaissance ideals of harmony and proportion. The painting represents both classical mythology and Neo-Platonic philosophy, celebrating divine love and beauty. Created for the Medici family, it showcases Botticelli's unique linear style and remains one of the most celebrated representations of classical mythology in Western art.

Year Created
1484-1486
Medium
Tempera on Canvas
Location
Uffizi Gallery, Florence
Dimensions
172.5 × 278.9 cm
