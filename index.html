Renaissance Masterpiece

Mona Lisa

The world's most famous painting by Leonardo da Vinci. This enigmatic portrait captivates with her mysterious smile and masterful sfumato technique, creating a timeless work of art that has fascinated viewers for over 500 years.

Leonardo da Vinci 1503-1519

Post-Impressionism

The Starry Night

Vincent van Gogh's most iconic masterpiece depicting a swirling night sky over a French village. Painted in 1889 during his stay at an asylum, this work showcases bold brushstrokes and vivid imagination that have made it one of the most recognized paintings in modern art.

Vincent van Gogh 1889

Japanese Ukiyo-e

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

Katsushika Hokusai's iconic woodblock print from the 1830s showing a massive wave threatening boats with Mount Fuji in the background. This masterpiece revolutionized Japanese art with its bold composition and use of Prussian blue, becoming one of the most recognizable images in world art.

Katsushika Hokusai 1830-1833

Renaissance Fresco

The Creation of Adam

Michelangelo's iconic fresco painted on the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel. This masterpiece depicts the Biblical creation narrative from the Book of Genesis, showing God giving life to Adam. The near-touching hands have become one of the most iconic images in art history.

Michelangelo 1508-1512

Renaissance Mural

The Last Supper

Leonardo da Vinci's monumental mural painting depicting Jesus and his disciples at the Last Supper. Painted on the wall of the refectory at the Convent of Santa Maria delle Grazie in Milan, it captures the dramatic moment when Jesus announces that one of them will betray him.

Leonardo da Vinci 1495-1498

Renaissance Fresco

School of Athens

Raphael's magnificent fresco in the Vatican depicting the greatest mathematicians, philosophers, and scientists of classical antiquity. Plato and Aristotle stand at the center, surrounded by other great thinkers in an architectural setting that celebrates human knowledge and reason.

Raphael 1509-1511
